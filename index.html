Malaria

what it is:

Malaria is a serious infectious disease causedby plasmodium parasites, spread to humans through the bite of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes.

Common symptoms:

Fever, Chills, Headache, Sweating, Nausea, and Fatigue. Severe cases can cause anemia, Organ failure, orDeath if untreated.

Where it's Common:

Mostly in tropical and subtropical regions, especially Africa, South Asia, and parts of Southeast Asia.

Impact:

=> Causes hundreds of thousands of daeths each year, mainly among children under 5 and pregnanat woman.

=> Leads to lost school and work days, increasing poverty and slowing economic growth.

=> Strains healthcare systems in affected countries

Prevention & Treatment:

Using mosquito nets, repellents, indoor spraying, and prompt treatment with antimalarial medicines can prevent and control the disease.

Diabetes

What it is:

Diabetes is a chronic condition where the body cannot properly control blood sugar (glucose) levels due to problems with insulin (a hormone made by the pancreas).

Main Types:

=> Type 1: The body makes little or no insulin (usually starts in childhood).

=> Type 2: The body doesn’t use insulin properly (most common; linked to lifestyle and genetics).

=> Gestational diabetes: Occurs during pregnancy.

Commonj Symptom:

Frequent urination, excessive thirst, hunger, fatigue, blurred vision, and slow-healing wounds.

Impact:

=> Can damage the heart, kidneys, eyes, and nerves if not controlled.

=> Increases risk of heart disease and stroke.

=> Affects daily life and requires long-term care.

Management:

Healthy diet, regular exercise, monitoring blood sugar, and medicines or insulin help control diabetes.

Cancer:

What it is:

Cancer is a disease in which abnormal cells grow uncontrollably and can spread to other parts of the body.

Causes:

Genetic changes, tobacco use, unhealthy diet, infections, radiation, and exposure to harmful chemicals.

Common Types:

Lung, breast, blood (leukemia), skin, and colon cancer.

Symptoms

Unusual lumps, long-lasting pain, unexplained weight loss, fatigue, changes in skin or bleeding.

Impact:

=> One of the leading causes of death worldwide.

=> Affects physical health, mental well-being, and family life.

=> Treatment can be long and costly, impacting education and work.

Prevention & treatment:

Healthy lifestyle, avoiding tobacco, early screening, and treatments like surgery, chemotherapy, and radiation help control cancer.

Brain Tumour

What it is:

A brain tumour is a mass of abnormal cells growing in or around the brain. It can be benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous).

Causes:

Often unknown; may involve genetic changes, radiation exposure, or family history.

Common Symptoms:

Headaches, seizures, vomiting, blurred vision, difficulty in speech or movement, and memory problems.

Impact:

=> Affects vital brain functions like movement, thinking, and senses.

=> Can disrupt daily life, school, and work.

=> Severe cases can be life-threatening if untreated.

Treatment:

Depends on type and size—surgery, radiation therapy, and medicines are commonly used.

Heart Attack

What it is:

A heart attack (myocardial infarction) happens when blood flow to part of the heart is blocked, usually by a clot, causing damage to heart muscle.

Main Causes:

Build-up of fatty deposits in arteries (atherosclerosis), smoking, unhealthy diet, lack of exercise, stress, diabetes, and high blood pressure.

Common Symptoms:

Chest pain or pressure, pain in left arm/jaw, shortness of breath, sweating, nausea, and dizziness.

Impact:

=> Can cause permanent heart damage or death if not treated quickly.

=> Affects daily life and may limit physical activity.

=> Major cause of illness and death worldwide.

Prevention & treatment:

Healthy lifestyle, avoiding tobacco, regular exercise, and medicines. Quick medical help greatly improves survival.

Asthma

What it is:

Asthma is a chronic lung disease in which the airways become inflamed and narrowed, making breathing difficult.

Common Triggers:

Dust, smoke, pollen, cold air, exercise, infections, and strong smells.

Symptoms:

Wheezing, shortness of breath, chest tightness, and coughing (especially at night or early morning).

Impact:

=> Causes breathing difficulty and tiredness.

=> Can affect school, sports, and daily activities.

=> Severe attacks can be dangerous without treatment.

Management:

Avoiding triggers, using inhalers, and regular medical care help control asthma.

Tuberculosis(TB)

What it is:

Tuberculosis is a contagious bacterial disease caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis. It mainly affects the lungs but can also affect other body parts.

How it Spreads:

Through the air when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks.

Common Symptoms:

Persistent cough (more than 2 weeks), fever, night sweats, weight loss, chest pain, and weakness.

Impact:

=> Damages lungs and reduces breathing capacity.

=> Can be life-threatening if not treated.

=> Major public health problem in many countries.

Prevention & treatment:

BCG vaccination, good hygiene, and long-term antibiotic treatment can cure TB if taken properly.

Stroke

What it is:

A stroke occurs when blood supply to the brain is interrupted, either due to a blocked blood vessel (ischemic) or a burst vessel (hemorrhagic). This deprives brain cells of oxygen.

Common Symptoms:

Sudden weakness or paralysis on one side of the body, slurred speech, confusion, vision problems, severe headache, and dizziness.

Impact:

=> Can cause permanent disability (paralysis, speech loss).

=> A leading cause of death and long-term disability worldwide.

=> Affects independence and quality of life.

Prevention & Treatment:

Controlling blood pressure, healthy lifestyle, and quick medical treatment greatly reduce damage.

Key Note:

Stroke is a medical emergency—fast treatment saves brain function.

Arthritis

What it is:

Arthritis is a condition that causes inflammation of joints, leading to pain and stiffness.

Main Types:

=> Osteoarthritis: Wear and tear of joints (common with aging).

=> Rheumatoid arthritis: An autoimmune disease where the body attacks its own joints.

Common Symptoms:

Joint pain, swelling, stiffness (especially in the morning), and reduced movement.

Impact:

=> Makes walking, writing, and daily activities difficult.

=> Can cause long-term joint damage and disability if untreated.

Management:

Regular exercise, healthy weight, medicines, and physiotherapy help reduce pain and improve movement.

Hepatitis

What it is:

Hepatitis is a disease that causes inflammation of the liver, most often due to a viral infection.

Main Types:

=> Hepatitis A: Spreads through contaminated food and water.

=> Hepatitis B: Spreads through blood and body fluids.

=> Hepatitis C: Spreads mainly through infected blood.

Common Symptoms:

Jaundice (yellow skin/eyes), fatigue, nausea, abdominal pain, dark urine, and fever.

Impact:

=> Affects liver function.

=> Long-term infection (B and C) can cause liver damage or cirrhosis.

=> Can become serious if untreated.

Prevention & Treatment:

Vaccines are available for Hepatitis A and B. Good hygiene, safe medical practices, and proper treatment help control hepatitis.

Epilepsy

What it is:

Epilepsy is a neurological disorder in which a person has repeated seizures due to abnormal electrical activity in the brain.

Causes:

Genetic factors, brain injury, infections, birth-related problems, or sometimes unknown causes.

Common Symptoms:

Seizures (fits), brief loss of awareness, unusual movements, staring spells, or confusion after an episode.

Impact:

=> Can affect learning, safety, and daily activities.

=> Emotional and social challenges may occur due to unpredictability of seizures.

=> Many people live normal lives with proper treatment.

Management:

Regular use of anti-epileptic medicines, avoiding triggers (like lack of sleep), and medical follow-up help control seizures.
